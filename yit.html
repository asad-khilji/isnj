ISNJ Youth Islamic Talk (YIT) Competition 2023

Location: 11 Deerfield Pl FLanders, NJ

Last Date to Register: Oct 15th 2023; Last Date to submit write up : Oct 22nd 2023


———–Guidelines for the Youth Islamic Talk————


Welcome to the 2nd annual ISNJ Youth Islamic Talk competition. We invite youth from NJ Masjids to participate in one of its kind event designed to give our youth an opportunity to give talk on various topics rooted in Islamic Principles and win prizes!!


Please reach out to the organizers via text message to 973-337-3639 or to isnjyouth2@gmail.com email.


Youth Islamic Talk (YIT) is split between two categories –


Category 1 – Middle School students (students entering grades 6th, 7th, and 8th)


Category 2 – High School students (students entering grades 9th, 10th, 11th, and 12th)


Rules and regulations for both categories are the same.


An “entry” to the YIT will consist of an individual or a team of a maximum of two people.


Each entry will select a topic of interest from the list of topics provided below.


Each entry must register using the registration form provided below by the 15th of October,2023


Each entry will have a maximum of five minutes of talk time to present their research, thoughts, and ideas about their topic.


Each entry will be required to submit a draft of their write-up to isnjyouth2@gmail.com by no later than October 22nd, 2023.


There are a few different areas that an entry will be evaluated on. So, as you prepare the material for your topic, please keep the rubric for judging (attached below) in consideration.


No props, pictures, or projectors will be allowed to support your talk.


Note cards /flash cards are also not allowed. An Arabic/English Quran will be available for reference if you need to recite an ayah.


Category 1 – Middle School Topics


What does our deen say about our parents?


Stories from the Quran and lessons you learn.


Surah or ayat from Quran that inspires you and why


Learnings from the life of Prophet Muhammed (PBUH), what influences you the most, and how is that relevant in today’s world?


Scientific discoveries by Islamic scientists with guidance from the Quran.


Category 2 – High School Topics


Learnings from the life of Prophet Muhammed (PBUH), what influences you the most, and how is that relevant in today’s world?


Muslim Identity and LGBTQ+ crisis


How do our Islamic beliefs call us for unity and support each other?


The importance of time in Islam.


Finding guidance in the constantly changing world through Quran.


Stories from the Quran and lessons you learn?


What are the advantages and dangers of social media?


ISNJ Islamic Talks 2023. Rubric for assessment of talks delivered.


Communication 40%


Concise: Was the message delivered within the allocated time and with the least amount of redundancies?


Clarity: The speaker’s message is clear and easy to understand.


Engagement: The speaker engages the audience and keeps them interested.


Confidence: The speaker speaks with confidence and authority.


Content 40%


Accuracy: The speaker’s information is accurate.


Relevance: The speaker’s information is relevant to the topic at hand.


Depth: The speaker goes into sufficient depth on the topic.


Overall 20%


Overall impact: How was the speaker able to bring his/her communication skills and content together to give an integrated message? The speaker’s talk has a positive impact on the audience.


Overall presentation: The speaker’s presentation is well-organized and professional.


Here is a more detailed explanation of each of the criteria:


Communication


Timing: Was the message delivered within the allocated time?


Clarity: The speaker’s message should be clear and easy to understand. The speaker should use language that is appropriate for the audience and should avoid jargon or technical terms. The speaker should also be able to explain complex concepts in a way that is easy to understand.


Engagement: The speaker should engage the audience and keep them interested. The speaker can do this by using humor, stories, or other techniques to keep the audience’s attention. The speaker should also be able to answer questions from the audience in a clear and concise way.


Confidence: The speaker should speak with confidence and authority. The speaker should believe in what they are saying and should be able to communicate their message in a way that is convincing.


Content


Accuracy: The speaker’s information should be accurate and up-to-date. The speaker should do their research and make sure that they are providing accurate information. The speaker should also be able to cite their sources so that the audience can verify the information for themselves.


Relevance: The speaker’s information should be relevant to the topic at hand. The speaker should not go off on tangents or introduce irrelevant information. The speaker should focus on the topic at hand and provide information that is relevant to the audience’s needs.


Depth: The speaker should go into sufficient depth on the topic. The speaker should not just provide a superficial overview of the topic. The speaker should provide enough information so that the audience can understand the topic and its implications.


Overall


Overall impact: The speaker’s talk should have a positive impact on the audience. The speaker should leave the audience with a better understanding of the topic and a new perspective on the issue. The speaker should also inspire the audience to take action.


Overall presentation: The speaker’s presentation should be well-organized and professional. The speaker should use visuals, handouts, or other materials to support their presentation. The speaker should also be able to handle any questions or challenges from the audience in a professional and respectful manner.
